Williamsburg Waterfront High Rise

In Progress

About the Project

A-Line Studio is currently upgrading and renovating the lobby and corridors to keep this older Williamsburg high rise competitive with the cool new neighbours.

My Role

We were initially hired to provide concept renderings for the lobby and resident corridors. The concepts were primarily for marketing purposes with the goal to eventually implement the design concepts. The property is one of the first high rises to have been built on this stretch of waterfront and it needed a renovation to stay competitive with the new neighbouring buildings. After seeing the renderings our client is now ready to begin working towards bringing them to life in their own building.

Project Process

Still in the design phase, the potential for this building is exciting. I started by considering the surrounding geography, looking at the competition, and also listening to what the board liked and didn’t like about those other buildings, with the aim being to create a welcoming and upscale building while also striking a balance between being on trend and timeless I developed a mood board with inspiration images and colors to gauge the client’s taste.

We were given a lot of creative freedom to develop a concept. We communicated rough ideas through simple SketchUp renderings to get feedback before sending the final concept to a renderer.

The building receives a lot of natural light and has lots of large windows. Considering their placement right on the water I felt it was fitting to bring as much nature inside as we could. We added a green wall and divided and highlighted it with warm wood columns. To counterbalance the organic elements we designed a minimal and sleek geometric inspired concierge desk.

Keeping in mind that storage for packages is a big concern for them, we reorganized the space to create more storage room by pushing the desk forward and tucking it within a niche that has storage room access behind it. The niche is clad in lightly stained oak and the reveals between wood veneer panels create a subtle geometric pattern.

In the corridors we developed a custom broadloom carpet with a soft and simple pattern. Wanting to stay modern and minimal but also warm. We kept the design simple, organic and fluid with no clear repeat. The yarn colors were hand selected to create contrast while also steering clear of large areas of solid color, in order to help hide dirt. The walls are clad in a wood veneer wall covering the carries onto the ceiling, recalling the wood niche downstairs. Tones relate back to the warmth of the natural wood in the lobby. Lighting is kept modern and simple.
